## Problem

Compute placement is static and complex, while cost, energy, and carbon change continuously.

Enterprises face a cloud compute trilemma:

1. Cost: public cloud pricing is volatile, on-demand infrastructure creates unpredictable bills, GPU and AI workloads amplify cost exposure, and customers often face 3x-4x price variance between providers and regions.
2. Carbon and energy: AI is driving exponential compute demand, energy costs are rising, grid carbon intensity varies by region and time, and most cloud platforms report emissions after the fact instead of optimizing workload placement in real time.
3. Control and complexity: private cloud can reduce cost but is complex to operate, hybrid and multicloud environments create overhead, enterprises need compliance and sovereignty, and cloud teams face growing technical friction.

Hidden crisis: cloud is becoming a critical utility, but its operating model is not optimized for the energy-constrained AI era.

The market is moving from "where can I run compute?" to "where should this workload run right now?"
