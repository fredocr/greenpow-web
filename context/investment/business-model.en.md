# Investment Business Model

## Entry Model

Public / distributed compute:

- Self-service workloads.
- Developers, AI builders, web apps, APIs, agents.
- Usage-based pricing.
- Lower ARPU but faster adoption.
- High utilization potential.
- Product-led growth motion.

## Expansion Model

Private Cloud as a Service:

- Contracted infrastructure deployments.
- Enterprise recurring revenue.
- Higher ACV.
- Compliance-driven demand.
- Data sovereignty guarantee.
- Managed-service relationship.
- Stronger revenue predictability.

## Shared Control Layer

The same control layer supports both segments.

This matters because:

- Public compute creates early usage and utilization data.
- Private cloud creates larger contracted revenue.
- The control layer improves margin, utilization, customer value, and defensibility across both segments.

## Revenue Logic

GREENPOW revenue scales with:

- Deployed compute capacity.
- Utilization rate.
- Enterprise/private cloud mix.
- Higher-value GPU workloads.
- Optimization and management layer.
- Recurring contracts.
- Infrastructure partner supply.

Investor-safe line:

> GREENPOW's revenue potential scales with deployed capacity and utilization, while the control layer increases margin, differentiation, and customer retention.

## Capacity Scaling

GREENPOW's revenue scales with deployed compute capacity and utilization.

Key deck context:

- 100 kW deployment can prove commercial demand.
- 1 MW deployment scales operating leverage.
- Seed establishes the platform.
- Series A expands capacity toward 5 MW.

## GPU / AI Compute Expansion

GPU matters because:

- GPU workloads have higher price variance.
- GPU workloads have higher energy exposure.
- AI compute demand is growing quickly.
- Optimization upside is stronger where price and energy gaps are larger.

Investor-safe line:

> GREENPOW's initial infrastructure model validates cost and carbon-aware orchestration, while GPU workloads create a larger future optimization opportunity because they are more energy-sensitive and price-variable.

## Payback Context

Deck context:

- 5 MW reaches approx. 1.8-year payback at approx. 60% utilization.
- Utilization improves capital recovery, margin profile, and infrastructure economics.
- Larger capex is Series A / later-stage territory.

Do not frame full 5 MW ownership capex as Seed-funded. Seed supports readiness and early expansion.
