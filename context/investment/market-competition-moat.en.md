## Competitive Landscape

### Hyperscalers

Examples: AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud.

Strengths:

- Scale.
- Reliability.
- Enterprise trust.
- Global regions.
- Deep ecosystem.

Weaknesses / gaps:

- Complex pricing.
- Lock-in.
- Limited workload-level emissions control.
- Emissions dashboards are often reporting-focused.
- Limited real-time customer-side orchestration based on carbon and energy.
- Less suitable for certain sovereign or local infrastructure needs.

### EU / Sovereign Cloud And IaaS Providers

Examples: OVHcloud, Hetzner, Scaleway, regional data centers.

Strengths:

- Locality.
- Lower cost than hyperscalers.
- Sovereign positioning.
- Fixed-price infrastructure.

Weaknesses / gaps:

- Limited carbon-aware workload orchestration.
- Limited integrated Scope 2 reporting.
- Less automation.
- Less dynamic placement intelligence.

### Private And Managed Cloud Providers

Examples: IFX Networks, H1O, regional integrators and MSPs.

Strengths:

- Customer relationships.
- Managed service experience.
- Reliability.
- Regional presence.

Weaknesses / gaps:

- Limited automation-first model.
- Limited sustainability-native orchestration.
- Often weaker user experience.
- Lower productization.

### Hosting And Edge Transition Players

Examples: Vercel, Hostinger, legacy hosting providers, edge infrastructure platforms.

Strengths:

- Developer experience.
- Simplicity.
- Fast onboarding.

Weaknesses / gaps:

- Limited infrastructure control.
- Limited carbon-aware routing.
- Not designed as sovereign/private cloud control layers.
