# Investor Content Rules

Use these rules whenever generating GREENPOW investor-facing pages, data room pages, fundraising pages, pitch copy, metric cards, or investor FAQs.

## Tone

Use:

- Precise.
- Investor-grade.
- Technical but readable.
- Evidence-based.
- Confident but not exaggerated.
- Cost and infrastructure first.
- Climate as measurable advantage.

Avoid:

- Hype.
- Unverified climate claims.
- Buzzword-heavy AI language.
- "We are the only company."
- "Guaranteed."
- "Zero emissions."
- "Fully autonomous global cloud."
- "Carbon neutral cloud" unless backed by certification.

## Evidence Labels

Every metric must be labeled as one of:

- Achieved.
- Observed.
- Measured.
- Modeled.
- Projected.
- Target.
- Benchmark.
- R&D validated.
- Investor estimate.

## Correct Framing

Say:

- Carbon-aware orchestration.
- Workload placement.
- Scope 2 emissions tracking.
- Distributed compute.
- Private cloud as a service.
- Cost, carbon, latency, and compliance optimization.
- Routing layer for global compute.

Do not overuse:

- Green cloud.
- Carbon neutral.
- Blockchain.
- Token.
- Decentralized cloud.
- AI-powered without explanation.

## Red Flags To Avoid

- Do not make vague sustainability claims.
- Do not claim AI-powered unless tied to forecasting, scheduling, optimization, or decision logic.
- Do not imply every workload gets the same carbon reduction.
- Do not say GREENPOW will replace AWS, Azure, or Google everywhere.
- Do not make 5 MW, 2030 revenue, or global routing sound already achieved.
- Do not leave the business model unclear.
- Do not make token language central to the enterprise revenue story.
- Do not create high-capex fear; explain staged deployment and utilization logic.
- Do not lead only with climate; lead with cost, complexity, compliance, and operational value.
- Do not make unsupported hyperscaler comparisons.
